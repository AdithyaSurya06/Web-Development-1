MITHALI RAJ

EARLY LIFE | CRICKET CAREER | RECORDS | AWARDS | OUTSIDE CRICKET | REFERENCE

One of the most inspirational biographies for youth

EARLY LIFE

Mithali Raj was born on 3 December 1982 in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, into a Tamil-speaking family.
Her father, Dorai Raj, was an officer in the Indian Air Force.
Mithali began playing cricket at the age of 10.
She studied at Keyes High School and Kasturba Gandhi Junior College in Hyderabad.
She trained in cricket from a young age along with her elder brother.

CAREER

Domestic Career

Mithali Raj played for Air India Railways domestic cricket with players like Anjum Chopra.

International Career

Mithali debuted in ODIs in 1999 with a score of 114*.
She scored 214 in a Test in 2002, setting a world record.
Played in 2005, 2017, and 2022 World Cups.
She retired from T20Is in 2019 and all formats in 2022.

Show Records and Awards

RECORDS

  • Nicknamed "Lady Tendulkar".
  • All-time leading run-scorer for India across all formats.
  • First woman to score 7 consecutive ODI fifties.
  • Played 109 consecutive ODIs.

AWARDS

  1. Arjuna Award (2003)
  2. Padma Shri (2015)
  3. Youth Sports Icon (2017)
  4. Khel Ratna (2021)

Outside Cricket

Mithali is a trained Bharatanatyam dancer.
A movie Shabaash Mithu was made on her life featuring Taapsee Pannu.
Released on 15 July 2022 but failed commercially, grossing only ₹2.88 crore from a budget of ₹30 crore.
